Brown noise (also called red noise) has a deeper, warmer sound than white noise. It emphasizes lower frequencies and rolls off sharply at higher ones — similar to the sound of a strong river, heavy rain, or a powerful fan.

For focus and deep work, brown noise tends to outperform white noise for many people. The lower frequency profile is less fatiguing on the ears during long sessions, and the consistent texture blocks distracting thoughts without being intrusive.

Cognitive research suggests that moderate background noise — around 70 decibels — enhances creative and analytical thinking by inducing a mild level of distraction that paradoxically improves abstract processing. Brown noise sits naturally in this range.

In Neurial, you can mix brown noise with beta brainwave entrainment (13-30 Hz), which is associated with active thinking, focus and problem solving. Add a light rain layer on top and you have one of the most effective focus environments you can build with audio.

ADHD brains are wired differently when it comes to stimulation. The prefrontal cortex — responsible for attention, impulse control and working memory — tends to be underactive. This means ADHD brains often seek external stimulation to compensate.

Background noise, particularly brown and pink noise, provides a steady stream of non-distracting stimulation that helps regulate this underactivity. Many people with ADHD report that they concentrate significantly better with noise than in silence.

Brown noise is the most commonly reported effective frequency for ADHD. Its deep, consistent texture provides enough stimulation to satisfy the brain's need for input without triggering distraction. Pink noise is also effective for those who find brown noise too heavy.
